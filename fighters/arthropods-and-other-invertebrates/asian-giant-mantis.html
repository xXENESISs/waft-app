Asian Giant Mantis

Hierodula tenuidentata

Region: South and Southeast Asia

Habitat: Forest edges, dense vegetation, tropical environments and humid woodland zones

Martial Representation: Muay Boran

The Asian Giant Mantis is an aggressive close-to-mid-range predator built around violent interception, sharp limb control and sudden explosive punishment. Tall, predatory and mechanically efficient, it thrives on timing, pressure and brutal entry sequences that collapse the opponent’s structure before they can reset. In combat, it combines predatory patience with abrupt, punishing force.

Martial Discipline Overview

Muay Boran is the ancient predecessor of modern Muay Thai, built for battlefield practicality and ruthless close-range efficiency. It emphasizes destructive elbows, knees, clinch domination, low kicks and forceful entries designed to break structure and overwhelm opponents under pressure. Within WAFT, it reinforces attack power, explosiveness and punishing close-range brutality.
