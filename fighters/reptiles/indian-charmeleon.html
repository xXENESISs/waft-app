Indian Chameleon

Chamaeleo zeylanicus

Region: Indian Subcontinent

Habitat: Dry forests, scrublands, open woodlands and vegetated lowland environments

Martial Representation: Bando with two Dha-hmyaungs

The Indian Chameleon embodies stealth, angular precision and deceptive body control. Measured, elusive and highly adaptive, it thrives through misdirection, sudden repositioning and sharp close-to-mid-range weapon sequences. In combat, it relies on timing, evasive intelligence and striking from unexpected lines rather than raw physical force.

Martial Discipline Overview

Bando is a traditional Burmese martial system built around adaptability, fluid transitions and practical combat efficiency. In the dual dha-hmyaung configuration, it emphasizes angular slashing patterns, deceptive rhythm and coordinated weapon flow from both sides of the body. Within WAFT, it enhances agility, technique and multi-angle offensive precision.

Combat Abilities

Passive Ability

Adaptive Camouflage

After using Concentration, the next incoming attack has only a 40% chance to hit.

If the attack misses, Chameleon gains +20% damage on its next attack.

Special Attack

Ballistic Tongue

Enters camouflage, reducing the opponent’s hit chance to 25% for that turn, then counterattacks with +50% Attack.

This counterattack cannot miss.

Ultimate Charge: Reactive (3 misses)
