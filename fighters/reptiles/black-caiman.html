Black Caiman

Melanosuchus niger

Region: Amazonia

Habitat: Rivers, flooded forests, swamps and tropical wetlands

Martial Representation: Huka-Huka

The Black Caiman embodies crushing grip strength, primal dominance and suffocating close-range pressure. Massive, armored and terrifyingly physical, it thrives through body control, explosive clinch engagement and relentless grappling force. In combat, it seeks to overwhelm, seize and break the opponent's structure before they can escape its range.

Martial Discipline Overview

Huka-Huka is an indigenous Brazilian grappling tradition from the Xingu peoples, centered on explosive entry, upper-body control and fast, forceful wrestling exchanges. It emphasizes raw physical engagement, balance disruption and immediate domination rather than prolonged striking. Within WAFT, it enhances clinch pressure, explosiveness and short-range grappling authority.
