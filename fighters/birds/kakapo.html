Kakapo

Strigops habroptilus

Region: New Zealand

Habitat: Native forests, mossy woodland and dense island vegetation

Martial Representation: Mau Rākau with Taiaha

The Kakapo embodies grounded authority, ancestral patience and deliberate weapon control. Heavy, unusual and deeply rooted in presence rather than speed, it dominates through measured structure, long-weapon discipline and calm positional superiority. In combat, it relies on reach, timing and committed control.

Martial Discipline Overview

Mau Rākau is a Māori martial tradition centered on weapon discipline, footwork, timing and embodied control. The taiaha, a long staff weapon used for striking, thrusting and feinting, requires precision and tactical rhythm. Within WAFT, it enhances reach, technical structure and controlled mid-to-long range pressure.

Combat Abilities

Passive Ability

Natural Mismatch

At the start of each round, Kakapo gains +10% to one stat and −10% to another. The affected stats can be Attack, Defense, Speed, Technique, Evasion or Explosiveness.

These effects do not stack. Each round replaces the previous buff and debuff with a new pair.

Special Attack

Total Chaos

Uses a random special ability taken from another animal.

It cannot miss, uses Kakapo’s current stats, and ignores any normal activation requirements.

Ultimate Charge: Offensive (3 hits)
