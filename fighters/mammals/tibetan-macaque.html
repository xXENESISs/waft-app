Tibetan Macaque

Macaca thibetana

Region: China

Habitat: Mountain forests, rocky slopes and highland woodland

Martial Representation: Hou Quan with Gun

The Tibetan Macaque embodies cunning mobility, deceptive rhythm and ferocious technical unpredictability. Tough, intelligent and physically crafty, it thrives through feints, abrupt changes of level and irregular timing. With the gun staff in hand, it becomes a chaotic yet disciplined close-to-mid range threat capable of disrupting structure before the opponent can settle.

Martial Discipline Overview

Hou Quan, or Monkey Kung Fu, is a Chinese martial tradition known for deceptive movement, sudden angle changes, low positioning and erratic timing. Combined with the gun staff, it gains extra range, fluid striking lines and continuous redirection potential. Within WAFT, it enhances agility, technical unpredictability and dynamic staff pressure.

Combat Abilities

Passive Ability

Persistent Harassment

Consecutive hits steal mana in increments of 5, up to a maximum of 30, and store it as loot. Missing an attack resets the streak.

Special Attack

Plunder Burst

Deals damage equal to loot ×2 and resets the counter. It cannot critically strike and cannot miss unless blocked by a protection effect.

Ultimate Charge: Offensive (3 hits)
