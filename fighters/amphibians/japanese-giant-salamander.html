Japanese Giant Salamander

Andrias japonicus

Region: Japan

Habitat: Cold mountain streams, rocky riverbeds and forested freshwater environments

Martial Representation: Tenjin Shinyo-ryu

The Japanese Giant Salamander embodies heavy rootedness, pressure tolerance and close-range disruption. Ancient, massive and physically unsettling, it does not rely on speed or elegant movement, but on composure, leverage and suffocating proximity. In combat, it thrives through stubborn contact, structural control and punishing exchanges at short distance.

Martial Discipline Overview

Tenjin Shinyo-ryu is a classical Japanese jujutsu school known for close-range control, kuzushi, immobilization and practical grappling pressure. It emphasizes balance breaking, body connection and efficient domination rather than ornamental motion. Within WAFT, it enhances clinch control, resilience and technical short-range pressure.
