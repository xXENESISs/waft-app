Japanese Fire-Bellied Newt

Cynops pyrrhogaster

Region: Japan

Habitat: Forest ponds, wetlands, slow streams and humid freshwater environments

Martial Representation: Tantojutsu

The Japanese Fire-Bellied Newt embodies hidden danger, deceptive calm and lethal close-range precision. Quiet, compact and visually restrained until it reveals its warning colors, it thrives through timing, short weapon efficiency and sudden directional commitment. In combat, it favors disciplined proximity, sharp interruption and calculated finishing pressure.

Martial Discipline Overview

Tantojutsu is a Japanese blade discipline centered on the use of the tanto, a short dagger optimized for close-range precision. It emphasizes timing, compact motion, controlled entry and efficient lethal targeting rather than wide or decorative movement. Within WAFT, it enhances short-range threat, technical sharpness and decisive finishing potential.

Passive Ability

Lethal Precision

Special Attack

Poisoned Dagger

An attack that uses Technique as its Precision value.

On hit, it injects Tetrodotoxin (TTX), applying −25% Evasion, −25% Technique and −25% Speed for 1 turn.

It also inflicts 30 fixed damage: 15 at the end of the current turn and 15 at the start of the next one.
